THE CHAMPASAK STORY
7 Must-Visit Spots

The Rose of Pakse :
7 Essential Stops

Pakse isn't just a destination; it's a living, breathing symphony of lifestyle and artistry. From Dao Heuang Market to the quiet rustle of Dong Hua Sao National Protected Area, explore the soul of the city.

"Pakse is not just a city at the confluence of two rivers; it’s where life flows at a poetic pace. To visit Pakse is to "go with the flow" and discover the true essence of Southern Laos."

Location 01

Dao Heuang Market

If you're looking for fresh local products at affordable prices, Dao Heuang Market is a must-visit. As one of the largest markets in Pakse, it’s a bustling hub where local vendors gather to sell everything from fresh produce, fruits, and seafood to meats and traditional handicrafts. You can even find electronics and home appliances here. For those crossing over from the Thai border in search of daily essentials, this market truly has it all.

DON'T MISS

Experience the charm of Dao Heuang Market: premium Bolaven Arabica coffee at local prices, exquisite hand-woven silks, and intricate silverware. Don't miss a taste of Khao Jee and Jaew Bong for a true glimpse into Pakse's authentic local lifestyle.

Location 02

Cathedral of the Sacred Heart

As a city once under French rule, Pakse still retains a charming display of colonial-style houses and architecture. The French influence also brought Christianity to the region, which is beautifully represented by an ancient church located in the heart of the city, not far from Champasak Hospital. This pale yellow, European-style building features a simple yet airy and elegant interior. It remains a central place of worship for Christians in Pakse and neighboring towns to gather for important religious ceremonies.

DON'T MISS

The charm of Sacred Heart Cathedral lies in its unique murals, depicting religious stories through local culture—featuring Jesus in Lao attire amidst the landscapes of Wat Phou. The cathedral’s simple yet powerful Western architecture beautifully reflects a faith seamlessly woven into Pakse's peaceful way of life.

Location 03

Champasak Palace Hotel

Champasak Palace Hotel is far more than just a luxury hotel in Pakse; it is a landmark steeped in history. Originally commissioned by Prince Boun Oum, the last Prince of Southern Laos, the building was intended to be his royal residence. Construction began in 1969, but before it could be completed, the Prince went into exile in France. The Laotian government eventually finished the project and transformed the former palace into the grand hotel that guests can enjoy today.

DON'T MISS

The charm of Champasak Palace, known as the 'Hall of a Thousand Rooms', lies in its elegant French-Lao architecture. Once a royal residence by the Sedone River, this majestic landmark is now a luxury hotel, beautifully preserving Pakse's grand history and heritage.

Location 04

Wat Phou

Situated along the banks of the Mekong River near Pakse, Wat Phou is one of the country's most significant ancient sites. Dating back to the 11th–13th centuries, this site is over 1,000 years old and features stunning Khmer architecture crafted from sandstone and brick. The temple complex still retains its original layout and is adorned with intricate ancient carvings. Nestled amidst lush green forests and majestic mountains, the breathtaking beauty of Wat Phou earned its status as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2001.

DON'T MISS

The charm of Wat Phou lies in its ancient Khmer architecture at the foot of Mount Kao. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, its sacred layout and stone walkways reflect a thousand-year-old civilization. This spiritual landmark remains a powerful symbol of faith and grandeur for the people of Champasak.

Location 05

Wat Luang

Wat Luang, officially known as Wat Poratana Sadsadaram, is a historic temple in Southern Laos located in the heart of Pakse along the banks of the Sedone River. Established in 1935, the temple features a unique blend of traditional Lao and modern artistic styles. It holds great significance as the first Buddhist school in Champasak. Visitors are drawn to the main ordination hall (Sim), which boasts exquisite wood carvings on its doors and windows. The temple grounds also house a historic library building and a riverside structure dedicated to Buddhist studies for monks.

DON'T MISS

Wat Luang is famed for its exquisite Ho Tham and traditional Southern Lao stuccowork. As a historic Buddhist study center by the Sedone River, it houses ancient wooden chapels and sacred images, reflecting Champasak’s deep spiritual legacy.

Location 06

Wat Phou Salao

Wat Phousalao is perched on a hillside near the Lao-Nippon Bridge. From the bridge, you can see a majestic golden Buddha statue standing elegantly against the cliffside. The area around the Great Buddha is also one of Pakse's most stunning viewpoints, offering a breathtaking panoramic vista of the Mekong River stretching as far as the eye can see, the Lao-Nippon Bridge, and the peaceful cityscape of Pakse. The temple grounds are also home to numerous smaller Buddha statues lined up beautifully. It is truly a must-visit first stop when arriving in Pakse.

DON'T MISS

The charm of Wat Phousalao lies in its majestic Golden Buddha overlooking the city. As Pakse’s finest panoramic viewpoint, it offers a breathtaking vista of the Mekong River and the Lao-Nippon Bridge, reflecting the deep spiritual faith of Champasak’s people.

Location 07

Dong Hua Sao

Dong Hua Sao National Protected Area in Champasak Province is a lush, fertile evergreen forest situated on the Bolaven Plateau, spanning across the districts of Pakse, Paksong, and Pathoumphone. It serves as a vital watershed and a sanctuary for rare wildlife, such as the yellow-cheeked gibbon. Beyond its rich biodiversity, the park is a premier eco-tourism hub, home to the famous Tad Fane and Tad Yuang waterfalls, attracting travelers to experience the grandeur of nature and adventurous activities amidst the verdant forest canopy.

DON'T MISS

The grandeur of Dong Hua Sao National Park lies in Tad Fane, Southern Laos’ highest twin waterfalls. Nestled in the lush Bolaven Plateau, this biodiverse haven is home to rare yellow-cheeked gibbons. For a thrill, the zip-line offers a breathtaking bird’s-eye view of Champasak’s magnificent and pristine ecosystem.
